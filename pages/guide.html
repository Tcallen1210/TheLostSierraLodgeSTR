The Lost Sierra is a very special place where time slows down, roots run deep, and natural beauty is in abundance. We hope you enjoy it as much as we do! What is the "Lost Sierra?" The "Lost Sierra" refers to a rugged and relatively untouched region in the northern Sierra Nevada of California and is known for its dense forests, mountain peaks, pristine lakes, and small historic towns. The term "Lost Sierra" was coined and made popular by ski historian William Blakely in his book Lost Sierra; Gold, Ghosts & Skis and reflects its remote, somewhat off-the-beaten-path character, with a rich history that includes Native American heritage and mining days from the Gold Rush era. The region offers a haven for outdoor activities like hiking, camping, fishing, and skiing, and it is often considered a hidden gem for those seeking to explore nature away from more crowded tourist destinations. "Set amid beautiful mountain peaks and charming towns, the Lost Sierra is considered the geography within Sierra and Plumas Counties in California. Home to Graeagle, Portola, Quincy, Lake Almanor, Greenville, and Chester, as well as dozens of smaller towns ‚Äì Loyalton, Downieville, Sierraville, and more ‚Äì and picturesque areas, all peppered with parks, trails, and waterways ready for exploration." - DiscovertheLostSierra.com You can visit these links for more info: DiscovertheLostSierra.com The Lost Sierra - Sierra Buttes Trail Stewardship Welcome to the Lost Sierra - SFGate article by Julie Brown Davis Lost Sierra - Wikipedia.com
